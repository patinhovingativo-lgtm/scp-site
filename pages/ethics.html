ICONDUCT & BEHAVIOR — Articles 1.1 – 1.10
ART. 1.1Respect for All Personnel▶

All members — regardless of rank, clearance, division, or background — must be treated with basic professional respect. Personal attacks, degrading language, or targeted humiliation are prohibited in all Foundation spaces.

ART. 1.2Anti-Harassment Policy▶

Harassment — any repeated, unwanted behavior designed to intimidate or demean — is strictly prohibited. This includes targeted messaging, threats, public humiliation, and coordinated efforts to make any member feel unwelcome.

ART. 1.3Non-Discrimination▶

Discrimination based on nationality, ethnicity, gender, religion, or language is absolutely prohibited. The Foundation operates globally. Derogatory remarks — even as jokes — are violations.

ART. 1.4Language Standards▶

Slurs and hate speech are prohibited. Main channels operate in English for moderation purposes. General profanity in appropriate contexts is tolerated; language used as a weapon is not.

ART. 1.5Conflict Resolution▶

Disputes must be handled privately or through official support tickets — not in public channels. Airing grievances publicly to disrupt the server is itself a violation.

ART. 1.6External Conduct▶

Members who identify as Site-07 members in external spaces represent the Foundation's reputation. Raiding other servers, brigading, or public defamation of the Foundation may result in disciplinary action.

ART. 1.7Constructive Feedback▶

Criticism of Foundation policies is welcome through appropriate channels. Criticism that degrades into personal attacks against staff will be treated as a conduct violation.

ART. 1.8No NSFW or Graphic Content▶

Sexual content, graphic violence, gore, and NSFW material are strictly prohibited in all Foundation channels. Violation results in immediate action without warning. There are no exceptions.

ART. 1.9No Spam or Flooding▶

Repeatedly sending the same message, flooding channels with emojis, or degrading the signal-to-noise ratio of any channel is prohibited. Use designated channels for their intended purpose.

ART. 1.10No Unauthorized Advertising▶

Advertising other Discord servers, Roblox groups, or services without explicit written approval from Administration is prohibited, including DM advertising directed at Foundation members.

IIAUTHORITY & CHAIN OF COMMAND — Articles 2.1 – 2.10
ART. 2.1The Chain of Command▶

Orders flow from The Administrator and O5 Council downward through Site Director, Department Managers, Division Leaders, and to all personnel. Every member has a superior and is accountable to them. This structure is non-negotiable.

ART. 2.2Compliance with Lawful Orders▶

Personnel must comply with direct orders from superior-ranked members when those orders do not violate this Code. Hesitation or debate during an active operation is insubordination. If you believe an order is unethical, comply first and report afterward through official channels.

ART. 2.3Insubordination▶

Deliberate and willful refusal to comply with a legitimate order from a superior officer is a serious violation. Insubordination during a containment breach or active operation carries elevated penalties due to the risk it creates for all personnel.

ART. 2.4Abuse of Authority▶

Rank is a responsibility, not a license to abuse. Personnel in authority may not use their rank to demean, exploit, or unfairly punish subordinates. Staff members who misuse moderation tools will face disciplinary action from Administration.

ART. 2.5O5 Council Authority▶

The O5 Council holds near-absolute authority over all Foundation operations. Their decisions are final and not subject to public debate. Publicly undermining O5 decisions is a serious violation. O5 identities are classified above SC-4.

ART. 2.6The Administrator's Supremacy▶

The Administrator holds supreme authority over [SCP] Site-07. No decision by any other body supersedes a directive from The Administrator. Attempts to undermine or impersonate The Administrator result in permanent, unappealable removal.

ART. 2.7Rank Integrity▶

Personnel may not falsely claim a rank, clearance level, or position they do not hold. Rank fraud is treated with the same severity as impersonation.

ART. 2.8Promotions and Demotions▶

Rank changes are the exclusive authority of designated command personnel and Administration. Personnel may not self-promote or negotiate rank changes outside the official process.

ART. 2.9Legitimate Dissent▶

Disagreement with a command or policy is permitted when expressed through proper channels — support ticket or designated feedback areas. Public campaigning against administrative decisions is prohibited.

ART. 2.10Inter-Departmental Jurisdiction▶

Personnel may not give orders to members of a different department unless they hold a cross-departmental authority designation. A Security officer does not command Research personnel; Research does not direct MTF units during operations.

IIIOPERATIONAL CONDUCT — Articles 3.1 – 3.10
ART. 3.1Containment Procedure Adherence▶

All personnel must follow established Special Containment Procedures for each active anomaly at all times. Procedures are not suggestions. Deviating without prior authorization from the Research Department is a violation.

ART. 3.2Unauthorized Breach of Containment▶

Willfully breaching containment of any SCP — for amusement, experimentation, or any unauthorized reason — is one of the most serious violations possible. Any unauthorized breach will be treated with maximum disciplinary severity.

ART. 3.3Emergency Protocol Compliance▶

When a breach, lockdown, or site emergency is declared, all non-essential personnel must immediately follow Emergency Protocol: evacuate designated sectors, cease non-critical activities, and follow MTF and Site Director instructions.

ART. 3.4Rules of Engagement▶

All armed personnel must operate within their designated Rules of Engagement at all times. Lethal force is authorized only under conditions specified in each division's ROE. Personnel may not expand their own ROE without authorization.

ART. 3.5No Random Deathmatch (RDM)▶

Killing any player without proper cause, authorization, or under Rules of Engagement is Random Deathmatch. RDM is prohibited in all forms against Foundation personnel, D-Class, allies, or hostile factions outside designated combat zones.

ART. 3.6D-Class Personnel Treatment▶

D-Class personnel are Foundation assets. While expendable under authorized protocols, they may not be terminated outside those protocols without cause. Unauthorized D-Class killing is a violation.

ART. 3.7Division Jurisdiction▶

Personnel must operate within the jurisdiction of their assigned division. Security guards do not conduct anomaly research. Researchers do not storm containment areas without authorization. MTF does not interfere with Research protocols during non-breach periods.

ART. 3.8Zero Tolerance for Exploiting▶

Using exploits, scripts, glitches, or unauthorized third-party tools is an immediate permanent ban from Site-07 and all affiliated platforms. No warning. No appeal.

ART. 3.9Operation Attendance▶

Personnel who formally sign up for scheduled operations are expected to attend. Repeated no-shows without notification will be treated as dereliction and may result in division demotion. LOA requests must be submitted via ticket before the absence.

ART. 3.10Inactivity Policy▶

Personnel inactive for 14 or more consecutive days without an approved Leave of Absence will be demoted or removed from their division. Returning from an unauthorized absence does not automatically restore previous rank.

IVINFORMATION & CONFIDENTIALITY — Articles 4.1 – 4.10
ART. 4.1Clearance-Gated Information▶

Foundation information is classified by clearance level. Personnel may only access, discuss, or distribute information at or below their own clearance level. When in doubt, assume information is above your clearance until confirmed otherwise.

ART. 4.2Prohibition on Leaking▶

Leaking internal Foundation information to external parties or unauthorized individuals constitutes treason. Treason results in immediate permanent blacklisting with no appeal process.

ART. 4.3O5 Identity Confidentiality▶

The identities of individual O5 Council members are classified above SC-4. No personnel below SC-4 may attempt to investigate, disclose, or confirm O5 identities. Unauthorized disclosure is a Level-5 infraction.

ART. 4.4Content Ownership▶

All creative content, lore documents, and official announcements produced within Foundation channels are the intellectual property of [SCP] Site-07. Distribution or reproduction requires written approval from Administration.

ART. 4.5No Impersonation▶

Impersonating any Foundation personnel — including staff, O5 Council members, or The Administrator — is grounds for immediate permanent removal. This applies to username mimicry, copying profile images, or falsely claiming to speak on behalf of others.

ART. 4.6Lore Canonization Authority▶

Only the Creator of Lore & Documents — in coordination with the Lore Department — holds authority to declare content canonical. Personnel may not present unofficial content as canonical Foundation lore.

ART. 4.7Document Integrity▶

Official Foundation documents may not be altered or forged by unauthorized personnel. Falsifying records, submitting false reports, or fabricating evidence in a disciplinary proceeding is a severe violation.

ART. 4.8Hostile Faction Intelligence▶

Sharing any Foundation operational intelligence with hostile factions is considered an act of war against the Foundation. This carries the maximum penalty regardless of context or claimed justification.

ART. 4.9Recording Policy▶

Recording or screenshotting Foundation internal communications and distributing them externally is prohibited. Evidence of violations may be submitted to staff via ticket, but public posting of internal communications is itself a violation.

ART. 4.10Data Retention on Departure▶

Personnel who leave the Foundation retain no right to Foundation content or materials accessed during their tenure. Continued distribution of Foundation intellectual property after departure is a violation of this Code.

VENFORCEMENT & DISCIPLINARY ACTION — Articles 5.1 – 5.10
ART. 5.1Disciplinary Authority▶

Disciplinary actions may only be issued by personnel with Staff designation or higher. All actions must be logged. The issuing staff member may not serve as the sole reviewer of an appeal. The Administrator and O5 Council hold final disciplinary authority.

ART. 5.2Verbal Warning▶

A Verbal Warning is the first-tier response to minor infractions. It is logged in the personnel file. Three verbal warnings for similar infractions automatically escalate to a Formal Warning.

ART. 5.3Formal Warning▶

A Formal Warning is an official documented violation recorded in the personnel file. Formal warnings are reviewed during rank evaluations and can affect promotions. Two formal warnings trigger mandatory review by Department Management.

ART. 5.4Timeout▶

A Timeout is a temporary removal from server communications. Duration ranges from 10 minutes to 7 days depending on severity. Attempting to circumvent a timeout using alternate accounts results in automatic escalation to a ban.

ART. 5.5Demotion▶

Demotion is the reduction of rank or clearance level as a disciplinary measure. It may be issued standalone or combined with other actions. Demotions require approval from the relevant Department Manager or Site Director.

ART. 5.6Suspended / Blacklist Status▶

Suspended status is a temporary revocation of operational privileges. Blacklist status is a permanent revocation. Members on the Blacklist lose all roles and are barred from participating in operations. Blacklist status is visible in the public hierarchy.

ART. 5.7Kick from Server▶

A Kick is the forced removal of a member from the Discord server. Kicked members may rejoin unless a ban is subsequently issued. A kick serves as a final warning before a ban. The reason for every kick is logged.

ART. 5.8Permanent Ban▶

A Permanent Ban is the most severe standard disciplinary action. Banned members are removed and blocked from rejoining. Bans are issued for critical violations including NSFW content, impersonation, exploiting, and treason.

ART. 5.9The Appeals Process▶

Any member who believes a disciplinary action was issued in error may submit an appeal via support ticket within 72 hours of the action. Appeals are reviewed by a senior staff member not involved in the original action. Bans from exploiting and treason carry no right of appeal.

ART. 5.10Amendments to This Code▶

This Code of Ethics may be amended by the O5 Council or The Administrator at any time. All amendments take effect immediately upon announcement. Personnel are responsible for staying current with this Code.
